our
associates

Sara Bullimore

Sara was the Artistic Director of the Lincoln Book Festival for seven years and her main interests and skills lie within the dance, theatre and literature sectors.

Since 2009 Sara has been working as a freelance consultant, undertaking many different projects in fundraising, event management, teaching and business management across all areas of the arts, including literature festivals and the Cultural Olympiad programme for Lincolnshire.

Heather Maitland

Heather specialises in business planning, marketing and audience development in the cultural sector.

She has delivered training programmes, keynotes and seminars throughout the UK and worldwide, most recently for the British Council in Colombia and Syria. She is an Associate Fellow of the Centre for Cultural Policy Studies at the University of Warwick.

Sue North

Sue North is a former associate editor of an award-winning regional daily paper, so has a thorough understanding of what the media wants and needs and knows exactly how to deliver it to them.

Her skills include writing, editing, design and proofreading and she also delivers media training. She particularly enjoys working in the arts and cultural sectors, where she has achieved extensive national and local coverage in print, broadcast and online for the festivals and events she works with.

Leanne Taylor

Leanne has worked in the creative and cultural industries for over 12 years planning events and festivals and developing participatory programmes with a range of communities.

She has a wide experience of Local Authority cultural development, including developing new creative programmes to address social inclusion, cultural tourism and crime and disorder.

Tim Harris

Head of Arts at Nottinghamshire
County Council for over a decade.

Tim works with independent arts organisations, local authorities, artist networks and the third sector. He has experience in arts and health, evaluation, project management, strategic planning and organisational development for the independent arts sector, commissioning and festivals.

Emma Tatlow

Working in destination management and marketing is Emma's passion...

Starting her working life with Holidaybreak, Emma then moved into marketing and has since spent a number of years working in UK destinations creating and delivering marketing strategies across public/private sector partnerships.

Emma is an advocate for the visitor economy concept bringing together all aspects of a place - culture and festivals, retail, nightlife, food and drink, accommodation, attractions to tell a compelling story to people who may live, visit, work or invest in a destination...

Nicki Jarvis

Nicki combines a practical arts background with 15 years of arts and cultural development as a museums and gallery curator and arts development officer.

She has a particular flair for writing funding applications alongside that of project development. Nicki particularly enjoys working with voluntary organisations and individuals, offering balanced support to assist projects from 'a twinkle in the eye' stage, to successful fruition.

Adroit Economics

Jamie Fotheringham from Adroit offers specialist consultancy advice in economic development and regeneration, with particular expertise in the creative industries and local/regional development.

Clients include local and central government, regeneration partnerships and other public sector bodies, as well as the private sector.

Ran-Design

A graphic design studio based in the historic City of Lincoln, offering a bespoke graphic design service.

Dave and the team specialize in all types of printed media and provide a range of work for cultural solutions uk.
